NMIX

I began taking NMIX in Fall of 2019 while in a state of flux regarding my major. I was lucky to find NMIX, as it married my interests of Computer Science (my former major) and Entertainment Media (my current major). I've really enjoyed my experience so far and look forward to finishing out the NMI certificate.

NMIX 4310

I'd be lying if I said this wasn't a challenging class for me. This semester marked the first time I've used a Macintosh computer for longer than 5 minutes. The learning curve was a little steep and frustrating, but the results very rewarding. I have pride in the projects I've completed and submitted but really wish I had spent about triple the time I did on each. I regret not buying a cheap macbook at the beginning of the semester to allow me to work on assignments at home. I'm excited to take what I've learned forward and continue to develop at least a little bit in the Apple ecosystem.

Midterm Project

My midterm project was a rather simple news app. It connects to NewsAPI.org and allows a user to browse through news articles for a given region. The view displays a headline, a little blurb from the article, and a photo (if the article contains one). Interestingly, I managed to pick a really strange API which worked around 5% of the time. This error was noticed rather late in the development but I think adds an interesting layer to the app in that the user gets to experience news from a different corner of the World on each use.

I decided to address one of my greatest problems in life with this final project -- time management. I made a calendar app which allows the user to look through each month, select a day, and add a note. The user can come back to a day on which they've left a note and view the saved text. I've tried to use a lot of calendar apps on the market, but I've really disliked all of them and switched over to a paper agenda. The massive drawback that an agenda poses is lack of portability. The aim of this project is to combine the simplicity of an agenda with the portability of an app. The app I've produced is extremely simple, but that's what I needed. Even Google Calendar is a bit much for me -- there's just too many things to look at. My app is a very streamlined calendar app as there are only two actions available to the user: add note and view note.
